Positive Covid-19 Cases in the United States

Start scrolling to see how Covid-19 developed in the US.

January 21, 2020

- Officials in Washington state confirm the first case on US soil.
- At an emergency committee, WHO says that the coronavirus does not yet constitute a public health emergency of international concern.

March 11, 2020

- WHO declares the novel coronavirus outbreak to be a pandemic. WHO says the outbreak is the first pandemic caused by a coronavirus.

June 11, 2020

- The United States passes 2 million confirmed cases of the virus, according to Johns Hopkins.

August 23, 2020

- The FDA issues an emergency use authorization for the use of convalescent plasma to treat Covid-19. It is made using the blood of people who have recovered from coronavirus infections.

December 14, 2020

- US officials announce the first doses of the FDA authorized Pfizer vaccine have been delivered to all 50 states, the District of Columbia and Puerto Rico.

February 22, 2021

- The death toll from Covid-19 exceeds 500,000 in the United States.
